## Overview

A flexible, modular identity system for the Museum of Design Atlanta’s summer program. This speculative rebrand replaced static assets with a dynamic "voxel" system designed to energize a K-12 audience.

## The Challenge

The original branding felt disconnected: too childish for teens, yet too generic for kids. MODA needed a **visual language** that could:
*   Span a wide age range (6-18) without feeling patronizing.
*   Reflect the camp's focus on "building" and "technology."
*   Work across digital (web, social) and physical (t-shirts, signage) mediums.

## The Solution: A Voxel-Based System

I moved away from standard flat graphics and developed a **3D Voxel System** inspired by Minecraft and retro gaming. This wasn't just an aesthetic choice. It was a functional one:

### 1. Modular Typography
I designed a custom alphabet where every letter is built from the same grid of blocks. This ensures that any new headline feels instantly "on brand" while remaining legible.

### 2. "MODA Monsters" & 3D Identity
To add personality, I used the same voxel grid to generate a family of mascots. These "MODA Monsters" act as brand ambassadors. They add energy to social posts or guide kids through wayfinding signage.

I paired the blocky structure with a neon-adjacent color palette. The contrast between the rigid grid and the electric colors creates a sense of "organized chaos" that mirrors the creative energy of a design camp.

## Outcome

This project demonstrates how **systems thinking** applies to brand identity. By defining the rules (the grid, the voxel), I created a system where unlimited assets can be generated. This ranges from t-shirts to Instagram stories, all while maintaining 100% visual consistency.
